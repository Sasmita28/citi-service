Kansas City 311 Dashboard


Keeping a city running takes communication by it's people and action from it's leaders.

A project was undertaken to evaluate the types of issues our city faces, the frequency that they occur, and the efficiency of our public services to fix them. To do this, we incorporated data from the Kansas City 311 Call Center and used a set of relevant filters. The 311 call center receives all types of service requests for all of the cities' departments. We pulled in the data from the Opendata KC website and applied the filters that follow:

  • Year: This is the year a service request was created.
  • Status: This is the progress of the request resolution.
  • Source: This is the method by which the city was notified of the issue.
  • Department: This is the sector of city government that is tasked with resolving the issue.

Charts and visual aids were created to look at the average days needed to close on requests as well as the amount of calls made and the breakdown by department for each set of filters.
